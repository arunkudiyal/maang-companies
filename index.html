Second, Apply Even If You Are In Tier 2 City Or Tier 3 College:

Often students lose the opportunity to apply to the Maang companies because such giants restrict campus placements to only Tier 1 colleges. But that doesn't mean that you cannot be the next Pichai or Nadella.

It is crucial that students learn the power of networking early on. Don't wait to finish your degree to create a LinkedIn account. If you are reading this article, create a professional LinkedIn account and start following people who interest you. This will help you track various positions with the giants which are posted on their social media and website. It is essential that you interact and network with employees of the company in order to grab a future referral.

If you are a student of tier 2 or 3 colleges, do keep a lookout on the career portals of these companies. Often a direct application works more favourably than campus placements. Also, your forthcoming and willing to take an initiative will be appreciated.

Noted giant Amazon gets nearly 18-20 job applications per minute whereas Apple has only a 2-3% acceptance rate. Google receives almost three million job applications per year. So it is true that you need to be beyond genius to land a job here, but there is nothing that a little hard work cannot achieve. Learn about referral etiquette, connect with head hunters and keep upskilling yourself.

Third, Research about the company:

It helps if you know that the company's interests align with you. While a lot of information is already available in the public forum about these companies, it is important that you do some sort of in-depth research before applying for these companies. The work ethic, culture and expectations might vary and differ to a great extent between the tech giants. Here's where networking can help you make a decision. Speaking to new recruits and HR managers to gain more knowledge about the company.

Researching about the company before the interview is not enough. It is important that you keep abreast with all the developments. Typically these IT giants are dynamic with new development every quarter, if not every month. The time you spend in researching the company can increase your chances of selection by a large margin.

Researching reflects that the candidate is aware, interested and invested in being a part of the brand name. It also reflects your excitement about being a part of the brand name

Fourth, Get Hands-On Practical Training & Showcase your work:

Do you have a penchant for coding? If yes, then you are already one step ahead. To get hired into any one of the MAANGs, you need to know about data structure/algorithms. But this alone is not enough. You need to learn to showcase your work on a resume. This will make you a more viable candidate and help you apply for more companies than you had planned. It is important that you get hands-on practical training. It is not enough to just get theoretical knowledge.
